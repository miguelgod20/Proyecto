Between the 6th and 7th centuries, West Slavic tribes settled between the Vistula and Oder rivers.

They organized themselves into agricultural and defensive communities.

The Polani tribe established a center of power in Gniezno.

Mieszko I consolidated the territory through force and diplomacy.

966: Baptism of Mieszko I:

He introduced Catholic Christianity.

He prevented Germanic expansion.

He integrated Poland into medieval Europe.

A central administration and a national church were established.

Władysław I achieves reunification.

Casimiro III the Great:

Judicial reform.

Promotes trade and mining.

Protects the Jews (Statute of Kalisz).

1364: Founding of the University of Kraków.

1385: Union with Lithuania.

Poland becomes a regional power.

1410: Battle of Grunwald defeats the Teutonic Order.

1569 – Union of Lublin

The Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth (Poland + Lithuania) is established.

A vast, multicultural, and multireligious state.

Unique political system:

Elective monarchy

Great power of the nobility (szlachta)

Parliament (Sejm)

16th–17th Centuries (Golden Age)

Great cultural, scientific, and economic development.

High religious tolerance (rare in Europe at the time).

17th Century – Decline

Constant wars:

Against Sweden (The Flood)

Russia

Ottoman Empire

The liberum veto paralyzes the government (a single nobleman could block laws).

Loss of power and stability.

Contemporary Poland (1989–present)
1989

End of communism.

Peaceful transition to democracy.

1990s–2000s

Economic reforms.

Integration with the West.

2004

Poland joins the European Union.

21st Century

Democratic country, with internal debates on:

Rule of law

National identity

Relationship with the EU

Growing economy and important role in Central Europe.
